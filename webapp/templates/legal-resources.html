Frequently Asked Questions

Eligibility for free legal aid typically depends on your income, assets, and the type of legal issue you're facing. Most legal aid organizations serve clients whose income is below a certain threshold (often 125-200% of the federal poverty level). You can use our eligibility checker tool or contact a legal aid organization directly to determine if you qualify.

Our templates are designed to comply with general legal standards, but laws vary by jurisdiction. For a document to be legally binding, it must meet specific requirements such as proper execution (signatures), legal capacity of the parties, and sometimes notarization or witnesses. While our templates provide a solid foundation, we recommend consulting with an attorney to ensure your specific document meets all legal requirements in your jurisdiction.

To make the most of your consultation, bring all relevant documents related to your legal issue. This may include contracts, correspondence, court documents, police reports, medical records, photographs, or any other evidence related to your case. Also, prepare a timeline of events and a list of questions you want to ask. Having organized information will help the attorney provide more accurate advice during your limited consultation time.

Our "Know Your Rights" section provides comprehensive information on rights in various scenarios. If you don't find information specific to your situation, you can use our search function or browse by category. For personalized advice, consider using our free chat consultation to speak with a legal professional who can provide guidance based on your specific circumstances and the laws in your jurisdiction.
